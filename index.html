About me

I'm a student developer and Jefferson Scholar at the University of Virginia.

I study computer science and mathematics in the College of Arts & Sciences. Some of my current interests are full-stack web development, cloud infrastructure, and functional programming. As a passionate student of theory, I'm fascinated by the formalisms underlying the state of the art. But I'm also a builder; as such, I am most captivated by elegant solutions to problems of practical importance. I am driven to harness the incredible power of modern computing towards socially responsible ends.

Experience

My past experience includes working as a Civic Digital Fellow at United States Special Operations Command, where I built a deep learning based image inference pipeline, designed full-stack web applications, and managed cloud deployments on Kubernetes. I have also worked two summer internships with LEAD Technologies, developing iPhone and Android applications that leverage LEADTOOLS image- and document-processing capabilities.

You can learn more about my background by viewing my resume.

Interests and involvements
